Why baby-name numerology matters

The name a child carries shapes the energy they project — for life.

Every child arrives with a Chaldean Moolank — the root number from their day of birth. That number governs the qualities they're naturally inclined toward: leadership, sensitivity, exploration, structure, communication, beauty, depth, discipline or courage.

The name has its own vibration. Each letter has a Chaldean value 1–9; the sum reduces to a single digit and maps to a planet. When the name's number is aligned with the child's Moolank, the name strengthens their core qualities — they grow into their fullest expression with less inner friction.

When the numbers fight, the same child works harder than they need to. Their natural strengths are partially obscured. Most parents don't realise this is upstream of school confidence, friendships, and identity formation in the teen years.

Letters are summed, then reduced to a single digit. Example: "Aanya" = 1+1+5+1+1 = 9. Name number = 9 (Mars).

Step 2. Child's Moolank

The day of the child's birth is reduced to a single digit. Example: a child born on the 6th has Moolank 6 (Venus). A child born on the 19th has 1+9 = 10 → 1 (Sun).

Step 3. Compatibility check

The name number is compared against the child's Moolank. Universally favourable name numbers are 1, 3, 5, 6 and 9; numbers 4 and 8 are typically avoided in early childhood. The strongest possible match is when the name reduces to the child's own Moolank — that is full vibrational alignment.

Frequently asked

Common questions about baby-name numerology.

What is baby name numerology?

Baby name numerology is the practice of choosing a name whose Chaldean numerical vibration supports the child's Moolank — the root number from their date of birth. When name and Moolank are friendly, the name strengthens the child's natural qualities rather than fighting them.

Which numbers are good for a baby's name?

Names that reduce to 1, 3, 5, 6 or 9 are universally favourable. Numbers 4 and 8 are typically avoided in early childhood. The single most powerful target is the child's own Moolank — when the name reduces to the same number as the birth root, the energy is fully aligned.

Should we rename our child if the numbers don't match?

Renaming is a personal decision. Most parents prefer to know the energetic terrain rather than change the name. Our tool tells you exactly which qualities the current name strengthens and which areas may need active parental support. If the name fights the Moolank significantly, parents sometimes choose a slightly different spelling at the school-record stage — but we don't suggest respellings for children, because parental intention behind the original choice carries its own weight.

What are auspicious days for a naming ceremony?

Use dates whose digit-sum matches the child's Moolank. For a Moolank 1 (Sun) child, dates 1, 10, 19 and 28 amplify the Sun's energy for naming, annaprashan, school admission and other milestone events. Our tool surfaces these dates automatically.

Does the surname matter?

The full name (first + last, as the child is commonly called) carries the operative vibration. Our tool reads whatever combination you enter. If the family always uses just the first name socially, that's the most relevant input. If the full name is used in school records and official documents, run the check on the full name.

Is this the same as Pythagorean or Western numerology?

No. Pythagorean numerology assigns letters in alphabetical sequence (A=1, B=2, … Z=8). Chaldean assigns by sound vibration — A=1, B=2, C=3, D=4, but F=8 (not 6), O=7 (not 6). Cheiro's body of work, considered the foundational text, is strictly Chaldean. Most authentic Indian numerology practice uses Chaldean.
