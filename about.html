The System

Chaldean vs Pythagorean — why it matters

Most numerology apps use the Pythagorean system. We don't. Here's exactly what sets Chaldean apart.

☽ The Chaldean System

  • Assigns values 1–8 to letters only — 9 is considered sacred and unassigned
  • Originated in ancient Babylon; used by Cheiro, the most famous numerologist of the 20th century
  • Compound numbers (11, 14, 22 etc.) carry distinct symbolic meaning — they are not simply reduced
  • Based on the vibrational energy of planets as understood in Babylonian astronomy
  • Works with the name you use today, not just your birth name

Pythagorean System

The Pythagorean system is the more familiar Western approach — letters assigned 1–9 in sequence, compound numbers mostly reduced to a single digit. It is widely used, but numerologists who study both systems consider Chaldean more nuanced and more accurate for name analysis.

Neither system is "wrong" — but for the serious student of numerology, Chaldean offers a richer language. It is the system we use, without exception.

Why 9 is Sacred

In the Chaldean system, 9 is considered the number of completion and divine wholeness. It is never assigned to a letter — but when a name or date reduces to 9, it carries the full weight of that symbolism. This treatment of 9 as sacred, unassigned-to-letters is one of the most important distinctions of the authentic Chaldean approach.
