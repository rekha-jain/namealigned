FAQ

Common questions

What is Chaldean numerology?
Chaldean numerology is the oldest and most accurate numerology system, originating in ancient Babylon. Unlike the more common Pythagorean system, it assigns values 1–8 to letters (9 is considered sacred and not used). It was popularised in the West by Cheiro (Count Louis Hamon) in his Book of Numbers. We use Cheiro's original Chaldean mapping.
What is Moolank and Bhagyank?
Moolank (also called Driver or Psychic Number) is calculated from the day of birth only — it is your most powerful and unalterable number. Bhagyank (also called Destiny or Life Path Number) is the sum of all digits in your full date of birth. Together they define the two primary vibrations of your life.
How does name correction work?
Each letter in your name carries a Chaldean numerical value. The total reduced to a single digit is your Name Number. If this number is not friendly with your Moolank and Bhagyank, Cheiro describes "a continual sense of muddle" in your affairs. The free analysis flags exactly which numbers clash and why. The ₹199 report goes further — it suggests specific spelling variants with Chaldean scores so you can pick the best aligned version of your name.
