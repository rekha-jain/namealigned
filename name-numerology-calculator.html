How the Chaldean calculation works

Three numbers. One honest read.

Step 1. Name number

Each letter is assigned its Chaldean value:

ValueLetters
1A · I · J · Q · Y
2B · K · R
3C · G · L · S
4D · M · T
5E · H · N · X
6U · V · W
7O · Z
8F · P

Sum the values for every letter in the name, then reduce to a single digit. Example: REKHA = 2+5+2+5+1 = 15, reduce to 6 (Venus). Name number = 6.

Step 2. Moolank (root number)

Reduce the day of your birth to a single digit. Born on the 6th → Moolank 6 (Venus). Born on the 19th → 1+9 = 10 → 1 (Sun). Born on the 28th → 2+8 = 10 → 1 (Sun).

Step 3. Bhagyank (destiny number)

Sum every digit of your full date of birth, reduce to one digit. Born 06-02-1990 → 0+6+0+2+1+9+9+0 = 27 → 2+7 = 9 (Mars). Bhagyank = 9.

Step 4. Compatibility check

The calculator runs your name number against the Chaldean friendship matrix for your Moolank and Bhagyank, factors in the compound number (Cheiro's full table from Book of Numbers), and produces a single alignment percentage. Then it maps the result into one of three tiers: Strong (≥75%), Moderate (50–74%), or Low (<50%).

Frequently asked

Common questions about name numerology calculators.

What is a name numerology calculator?

A name numerology calculator converts the letters of your name into numbers using a numerology system (Chaldean or Pythagorean), sums them, and reduces the total to a single digit called the name number. That digit reveals the vibration the name carries — its planet, archetype and energetic qualities. Combined with your date of birth (Moolank and Bhagyank), it shows whether your name aligns with the energy you brought into the world.

How accurate is Chaldean numerology compared to Pythagorean?

Chaldean numerology is the older system — it dates back ~4,000 years to ancient Mesopotamia and assigns letters by their sound vibration. Pythagorean assigns letters by their alphabetical position. Most authentic Indian numerology practice — including Cheiro's foundational work — uses Chaldean. Practitioners report Chaldean gives sharper personality and timing readings.

Do I need my full legal name or just first name?

Use the name you most commonly go by. If your friends and colleagues call you by your first name only, that's the operative vibration. If your full name appears on official documents and you use it daily, run the calculator on the full name. You can test both — the result tells you which version aligns more strongly with your Moolank.

What is Moolank, Bhagyank and Name Number?

Moolank (root number) is derived from the day of your birth — it's reduced to a single digit and represents your core driver. Bhagyank (destiny number) is the sum of your full date of birth — it represents your karmic path. Name Number is the sum of the Chaldean values of your name letters, reduced to a single digit. The calculator shows how these three numbers interact.

Can numerology actually change my luck?

Numerology is a diagnostic tool, not a magic solution. It reveals the energetic terrain you operate on so you can make conscious choices — when to launch a venture, which partnerships will flow easily, which name spelling supports your strengths. Many users report tangible shifts after correcting a misaligned name spelling, but the work is yours.
